THE ECHOTHRYX

Scientific Name:

Oculus Exulthryx

Discovered by / at:

Recorded at The Archive of Souls within The Horizon in 32 BCE.

Family:

Necro-Soma, derived from 'Necro' (Death), and 'Soma' (Body). It was found the organism is a part of the a living corpse's soul, trapped within its psyche.

Habitat:

The Exul Void. A deep crimson blood-filled hollow cave within the center of the self. It is a place of wet, pulsing veins and iron-scented atmosphere within a corpse where the Echothryx thrives.

Appearance:

The Echothryx does not possess a solid form; it is the ghost of flesh. Its outer body is a translucent membrane, and pulses with a faint shiver due to its unstable state of emotions. Its primary feature is the Orb. The Orb is a visually impaired human eye, a sphere of massive dark cloudy greys and a blind iris. When it detects a foreign presence, it detects the hollow void of the inner psyche and follows its memory path. The twelve Vibra-cortis are long, tentacle-like limbs attached to its outer and inner body. They are needle-thin extensions of its nervous system that twitch to the creature's emotional state. In a calm state, the Vibra-cortis remain stable and aid the creature to detect outside soundwaves. Once the limbs touch a foreign object, it can lash out and spike to convey its frustrated, fragile emotional state.

Key Characteristics:

  • Ghostly drifting.
  • Sound Pulse Tracking/Echolocation.
  • Trauma Avoidance.
  • Fear Shaking.

What we know so far about THE ECHOTHRYX? The Echothryx was once a vital part of a human being. It was attached to the mind, acting as a bridge between a person's mind and vision. However, it was discovered that once its original human being experienced an extremely traumatic event, the connection snapped, separating Echothryx and its host body. Now, the Echothryx is visually impaired and trapped in The Exul Void, making it unable to visually detect its surrounding environment and objects. However, it is able to detect a foreign presence through soundwaves, also known as echolocation.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature The creature's only goal is to find its way back home. It wants to reattach itself to the human's vision and mind, explaining why once the Echothryx moves, the parasitic faint eyes in the Exul Void open as a guide to its home path. The Red Circle is the wound where it was once connected. The creature fears it because it represents the pain of being torn away from a safe haven. The Mouse acts as a faint echo of that lost connection. By clicking, you are sending out sound waves the Echothryx can follow. It follows these waves desperately, hoping to go back to where it was once belonged.
